**Jack Pitcher:** You get the idea. So what gives? My colleague, Heather Gillers, called it a post-earnings hangover. She wrote that the dip was at least in part a natural breather for semiconductors and other AI related stocks and those companies have been tearing up the charts. After Tuesday's declines, Intel, Qualcomm, and Micron were still up more than 60% over the previous month. When all was said and done, the iShares Semiconductor ETF, a benchmark for the U.S. chip industry, closed out the week down two-and-a-quarter percent. This week also brought some long awaited news for crypto enthusiasts. A bill called the Clarity Act would create a pathway for crypto to further upend traditional finance. It's important for crypto companies who want, you guessed it, clarity about the rules of the road for cryptocurrencies across U.S. regulatory bodies. But the bill was stuck in the Senate Banking Committee for months. A big issue was whether crypto exchanges like Coinbase and other firms could offer so called rewards that pay recurring fees to the holders of popular tokens. Banks argued against what they call loopholes, saying they would threaten their deposits. On Thursday, the committee finally approved a version of the bill and in the end, neither side got everything it wanted. Banks won some concessions and shares of the stablecoin issuer Circle Internet fell 2%. Meanwhile, shares of Coinbase and Robinhood jumped more than 5%. But as is so often the case in crypto, the high didn't last. At the end of the week, shares of Robinhood were flat and Coinbase was down 3%. If you know the Swedish company Klarna for one thing, it's probably buy now pay later, but that might not be the case for too much longer. Klarna is shifting its focus more towards something called fair financing. That's a longer term type of installment loan designed for big ticket items. And this week the company said that pivot is working. The new offering has taken off faster than expected with gross merchandise volumes more than doubling from last year. The new strategy has also helped Klarna gain market share from competitors like Afterpay and Affirm in the recent quarter. After reporting earnings, shares of Klarna shot up 20% on Thursday. It was a welcome change for shareholders. Klarna's stock had lost about two thirds of its value over the past year. The shares gave up some of Thursday's gains on Friday, but still ended the week nearly 5% higher. And now you know what's news in markets this week. ## 整体翻译

# What's News in Markets：Clarity Act、芯片股回落、Klarna 转向

**Jack Pitcher：** 各位听众好，今天是 5 月 16 日，星期六。我是《华尔街日报》的 Jack Pitcher，这里是 *What's News in Markets*。我们会回顾本周股市中最大的价格波动，以及推动这些波动的新闻。我们开始吧。

本周开局强劲，但最后收尾有些走弱。全球债券收益率上升正在削弱投资者对股票的兴趣，而油价仍在上涨，重新引发了市场对通胀的担忧。周五，美国三大主要股指都下跌超过 1%。从整周来看，道琼斯指数下跌 0.2%，标普 500 指数小幅上涨 0.1%，纳斯达克指数下跌 0.1%。

对 Intel、Nvidia、Micron 和 Qualcomm 这样的芯片股来说，这是非常动荡的一周。为了让你感受一下这种戏剧性变化，下面是我们本周报道中的一些原话。周一……

**Speaker 2：** 市场对人工智能的热情继续推动股市上涨。

**Jack Pitcher：** 周二……

**Speaker 3：** 半导体板块走势反转，使市场陷入停顿。

**Jack Pitcher：** 周三……

**Speaker 3：** 芯片制造商从昨日的回落中反弹。

**Jack Pitcher：** 你懂这个意思了吧。那么到底发生了什么？我的同事 Heather Gillers 把它称为“财报后的宿醉”。她写道，这次下跌至少部分是半导体和其他 AI 相关股票的一次自然休整，因为这些公司的股价此前已经涨得非常猛。

在周二下跌之后，Intel、Qualcomm 和 Micron 的股价相较前一个月仍然上涨超过 60%。最终，作为美国芯片行业基准的 iShares Semiconductor ETF 本周收盘时下跌 2.25%。

本周也给加密货币支持者带来了一些期待已久的消息。一项名为《Clarity Act》的法案将为加密货币进一步颠覆传统金融创造路径。对于那些希望获得监管清晰度的加密公司来说，这项法案非常重要——也就是希望弄清楚美国不同监管机构对加密货币的规则到底是什么。

但这项法案此前已经在参议院银行委员会卡了好几个月。一个重要争议是，Coinbase 等加密货币交易所和其他公司是否可以提供所谓的“奖励”，也就是向热门代币持有人支付持续性费用。银行反对它们所谓的“漏洞”，称这些安排会威胁到银行存款。

周四，该委员会终于批准了一个版本的法案。最终，双方都没有完全得到自己想要的东西。银行方面争取到了一些让步，稳定币发行商 Circle Internet 的股价下跌 2%。与此同时，Coinbase 和 Robinhood 的股价上涨超过 5%。

但就像加密货币领域经常发生的那样，这种兴奋没有持续太久。到本周结束时，Robinhood 股价基本持平，Coinbase 股价下跌 3%。

如果你对瑞典公司 Klarna 有一个印象，那很可能是“先买后付”。但这种情况可能不会持续太久了。Klarna 正在把业务重点更多转向一种叫做“fair financing”的东西。这是一种期限更长的分期贷款，主要用于大件商品。

本周，该公司表示，这一转型正在奏效。这个新产品的增长速度快于预期，平台商品交易总额相比去年已经翻了一倍多。新策略也帮助 Klarna 在最近一个季度从 Afterpay 和 Affirm 等竞争对手那里夺取了市场份额。

公布财报后，Klarna 股价周四大涨 20%。这对股东来说是一个受欢迎的变化。在过去一年里，Klarna 的股价已经损失了大约三分之二。周五，该股回吐了周四部分涨幅，但本周收盘时仍然上涨近 5%。
